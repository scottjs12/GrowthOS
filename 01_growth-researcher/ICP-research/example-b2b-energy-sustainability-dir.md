## Demographics

- **Title:** Director of Sustainability, EHS Manager, VP ESG
- **Age:** 35-50
- **Reports to:** COO or CEO
- **Company size:** 200+ employees, multiple facilities

## Psychographics

- Mission-driven but budget-constrained
- Needs to prove sustainability = profitability to keep their seat at the table
- Measured on reduction targets, not just reporting
- Politically aware — knows they need Operations buy-in to get anything done

## Behavioral Patterns

- **Discovery:** Industry conferences (GreenBiz, Sustainability Summit), LinkedIn (active poster), sustainability-focused newsletters
- **Evaluation:** Needs audit-ready data — estimates don't count
- **Decision:** Builds internal business case, needs COO/CFO sign-off
- **Communities:** LinkedIn sustainability groups, GreenBiz community, NAEM (National Association for EHS&S Management)

## Language Patterns (verbatim)

- "Our data is in 6 different spreadsheets across 4 plants"
- "I need numbers I can put in front of the board"
- "We have a target but no baseline to measure against"
- "The operations team thinks sustainability is a cost center"

## Jobs to Be Done

- Hit Scope 1+2 reduction targets with measurable, auditable data
- Build a sustainability narrative the board and investors can see
- Prove that energy reduction = cost reduction (align incentives with Operations)

## Switching Triggers

- Annual ESG report deadline approaching with no progress to show
- New regulatory requirement (SEC climate disclosure, EU CSRD)
- Board or investor pressure for concrete emissions data
- Customer RFP requiring sustainability certifications

## Objections

- "We don't have centralized data to even start"
- "Operations won't prioritize this — they care about throughput"
- "We've been doing this manually and it's been fine" (it hasn't)
- "I need to show results before I can justify budget"

## Messaging Implications

- Lead with "measure and prove" — not just "reduce"
- Audit-ready and board-ready language resonates
- Show how the tool bridges Operations and Sustainability teams
- Free baseline assessment is the perfect entry point
- Avoid pure cost framing — this buyer cares about compliance + credibility
