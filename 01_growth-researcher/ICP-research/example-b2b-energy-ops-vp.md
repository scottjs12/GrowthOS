## Demographics

- **Title:** VP/Director of Operations
- **Age:** 40-55
- **Compensation:** $150-250K
- **Company size:** 100-500 employees, $20M-$200M revenue
- **Industry verticals:** Automotive parts, food & beverage, industrial components, consumer goods manufacturing

## Psychographics

- Risk-averse — won't adopt anything that could disrupt production
- ROI-obsessed — every vendor pitch must pencil out in a spreadsheet
- Skeptical of "tech solutions" — has been burned by vendors who overpromise
- Trusted by C-suite to keep production running and margins healthy
- Values peer recommendations over marketing claims

## Behavioral Patterns

- **Discovery:** Researches solutions via peer referrals, industry events (IMTS, Hannover Messe), and LinkedIn (lurker, not poster)
- **Evaluation:** Asks for case studies from same industry vertical, wants to talk to a reference customer before demo
- **Decision:** Needs CFO buy-in for anything over $50K/year — arms themselves with ROI projections
- **Communities:** Industry Week, LinkedIn manufacturing groups, trade association forums

## Language Patterns (verbatim)

- "Energy is our second highest cost after labor"
- "I don't need a dashboard — I need my bill to go down"
- "Every vendor says 'AI-powered' but can't explain what the AI actually does"
- "Show me the ROI or don't bother scheduling a call"
- "We can't afford to shut down a line for some audit"

## Jobs to Be Done

- Reduce energy costs per unit produced without disrupting production schedules
- Give CFO a margin improvement story that doesn't involve headcount cuts
- Meet board-level sustainability mandates without adding staff or complexity

## Switching Triggers

- Energy bill spikes 15%+ quarter-over-quarter
- New ESG/sustainability mandate from board or major customer
- Competitor publishes sustainability report and they don't have one
- Utility announces rate increase or demand charge restructuring
- Current "energy management" is a spreadsheet that nobody updates

## Objections

- "We can't afford downtime for an energy audit"
- "Last vendor promised 20% savings, delivered 4%"
- "Our plant manager won't adopt new software"
- "We already have a utility contract — what's the point?"
- "I need something that works across all our plants, not just one"

## Messaging Implications

- Lead with ROI and specificity — not features
- Use "no downtime" and "no hardware" as objection-killers above the fold
- Case studies must be from same manufacturing vertical to build trust
- Avoid buzzwords ("AI-powered," "smart energy," "digital transformation")
- Free audit offer lowers commitment barrier for risk-averse buyers
