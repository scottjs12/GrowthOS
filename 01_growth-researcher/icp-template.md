# ICP Profile Template

> Universal template for defining Ideal Customer Profiles. Complete one per ICP segment. Minimum 2, maximum 5 per client.
>
> **Usage:** Copy the ICP Profile block (everything between the `---` dividers) once per segment into `clients/[client-name]/researcher/icp-profiles.md`. Number segments by value: ICP 1 = highest-value segment, ICP 2 = next, and so on. Every downstream decision — copy, ads, targeting, CRO hypotheses, CRM segmentation — is only as good as the ICP data feeding it. Do this first. Do it well.

---

## Why This Matters

Every agent in GrowthOS consumes ICP data:

- **Content Writer** pulls messaging angles, pain-point language, and identity cues directly from these profiles.
- **Paid** uses firmographics, channel strategy, and behavioral signals to build targeting and lookalike audiences.
- **CRO** sources hypotheses from pain points, objections, and conversion triggers documented here.
- **CRM** segments lifecycle flows, email sequences, and scoring models based on these profiles.
- **SEO** maps keyword intent to ICP language and search behavior.
- **Designer** matches visual tone to psychographics and identity.

A vague ICP means vague copy, wasted ad spend, and weak conversion rates. Be ruthlessly specific. "35-year-old ops manager at a 50-person SaaS company drowning in manual reporting" beats "business professional" every time.

---

## Instructions

1. Copy the profile block below for each ICP segment
2. Fill in every field — leave nothing as "[placeholder]" before routing to other agents
3. Be specific and use real language your customers use — not marketing jargon
4. Validate with real data: interviews, surveys, analytics, or sales call transcripts — never assumptions
5. Prioritize segments: ICP 1 = highest value (best LTV, best fit, most reachable), work down
6. Route completed profiles to: Content Writer (messaging), Paid (targeting), CRM (segmentation), Designer (visual tone), SEO (search intent)
7. Revisit quarterly — ICPs drift as your product and market evolve
