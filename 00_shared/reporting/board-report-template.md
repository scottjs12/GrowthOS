# Board Growth Report Template

> What the board wants: Are we growing efficiently? What's working? What's not? What are you doing about it? Keep it to 10 slides worth of content.

## Report Structure

```
QUARTERLY GROWTH REPORT
───────────────────────
Company:
Quarter:
Prepared by:
Date:
```

### Section 1: Executive Summary (1 slide)
- 3 bullets. That's it.
- Format: "[Metric] [moved X%] [because reason] [and we're doing Y about it]"
- Example: "MRR grew 18% QoQ to $X driven by enterprise expansion. Blended CAC increased 12% due to Meta CPM inflation — shifting 15% of budget to organic."
- Rule: Lead with the number. Board members read the exec summary and skim the rest.

Include: Which channels scaled, which got cut, which are testing next quarter.

### Section 5: Cohort Analysis (1 slide)
- Revenue cohort chart (monthly cohorts, 6-12 months of data)
- Show: Are newer cohorts retaining better or worse than older cohorts?
- Highlight: Are cohorts expanding (NRR > 100%) or contracting?
- Rule: This is the chart that tells the truth. Growing MRR with declining cohort quality = ticking time bomb.

### Section 6: Experiments & Learnings (1 slide)
| Experiment | Result | Impact | Learning |
|---|---|---|---|
| | Win/Loss/Inconclusive | +X% on [metric] | [Transferable insight] |

- Include: Number of tests run, win rate, biggest win, biggest surprise
- Rule: Board doesn't need test details. They need: "We ran X tests, Y% won, biggest win was Z."

### Section 7: Competitive Landscape (1 slide, quarterly)
- Who launched what
- Market positioning shifts
- Pricing changes in market
- Rule: Only include if something material changed. "No significant competitive movement" is a valid update.

### Section 8: Next Quarter Plan (1-2 slides)
- Top 3 growth priorities (ranked)
- Budget allocation plan (by channel, with QoQ changes)
- Key experiments planned
- Hiring plan (if applicable)
- Key risks and mitigation
- Rule: Every priority has a metric it moves and a timeline. "Improve retention" is not a plan. "Reduce M2 churn from 8% to 5% via onboarding optimization by end of Q2" is a plan.

### Section 9: Asks (1 slide)
- Budget approvals needed
- Headcount requests
- Strategic decisions required from the board
- Rule: Be specific. "We need more budget" vs. "We need $50K incremental monthly for Google Ads, projected to deliver 200 customers at $250 CAC, 8-month payback."

## Preparation Checklist
- [ ] All numbers reconcile with finance (do not surprise the CFO)
- [ ] Cohort data pulled from source of truth (not spreadsheet approximations)
- [ ] CAC includes fully-loaded costs (salaries, tools, overhead)
- [ ] LTV uses observed data, not projections
- [ ] Competitive section reviewed by Researcher
- [ ] Next quarter plan reviewed by PM and Strategist
- [ ] Asks are specific with expected ROI

## Rules
- Never present a number without context (QoQ change, vs. target, vs. benchmark)
- Bad news first. If churn is up, say it in the exec summary — don't bury it in slide 7
- Every chart should have a "so what" annotation — what should the reader conclude?
- Keep it under 10 slides. Boards that see 30-slide decks stop reading at slide 5
- Send the deck 48h before the meeting. Board members who read ahead ask better questions.
