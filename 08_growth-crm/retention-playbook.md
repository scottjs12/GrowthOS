# Retention & Churn Playbook

> Acquisition gets attention. Retention builds the business. A 5% increase in retention = 25-95% increase in profit. This playbook covers the tactics.

## Retention Framework: The 3 Layers

### Layer 1: Activation (Day 0-7)
Goal: Get users to the "aha moment" as fast as possible.

Tactics:
- **Welcome email** — Send within 5 minutes of signup. Single CTA: do the core action.
- **Onboarding checklist** — Show progress (3-5 steps max). Celebrate completion.
- **Time-to-value optimization** — Remove every step between signup and first value. If it takes >2 minutes, you'll lose 40% of users.
- **Behavioral triggers** — If user hasn't completed first action in 24h, send nudge email with walkthrough video or GIF.
- **First-session experience** — Pre-populate with sample data if the product feels empty. Empty states kill activation.

KPIs: First action rate (24h), onboarding completion rate, time-to-first-value

### Layer 2: Engagement (Day 7-30)
Goal: Build the habit. Make the product part of their routine.

Tactics:
- **Usage milestone emails** — "You've scanned 10 cards!" / "You've completed your first project!" Celebrate progress.
- **Feature discovery** — Introduce one new feature per week via tooltip or email. Don't overwhelm.
- **Variable rewards** — Surprise value (unexpected insights, achievements, discoveries). This is what makes apps sticky.
- **Social features** — If applicable: sharing, collaboration, community. Social ties increase switching cost.
- **Weekly digest** — Summary of activity + prompt to return. Keep it short — 3 bullets max.

KPIs: D7 retention, D14 retention, weekly active rate, feature adoption rate

### Layer 3: Retention (Day 30+)
Goal: Lock in the habit. Increase switching cost (ethically).

Tactics:
- **Data moats** — The more data users put in, the harder to leave. Encourage collection building, history, customization.
- **Integrations** — Connect to their other tools. Each integration increases retention ~15%.
- **Personalization** — Recommendations, custom views, saved preferences. Generic experiences don't retain.
- **Community** — Forums, user groups, ambassador programs. Peer relationships retain better than product features.
- **Loyalty/rewards** — Points, streaks, tiers. Works for consumer apps. Use sparingly for B2B.

KPIs: D30 retention, D90 retention, NPS, churn rate, expansion revenue

## Churn Signals (detect before they leave)

| Signal | Timeframe | Response |
|---|---|---|
| No login in 7 days | Week 2+ | Nudge email: "Here's what you missed" |
| Usage frequency drops >50% | Any week vs. prior | Feature highlight email + in-app prompt |
| Support ticket unresolved >48h | Any time | Priority escalation — frustrated users churn 3x faster |
| Billing page visit without upgrade | Day 14+ | Offer personalized demo or discount |
| Export/download all data | Any time | HIGH ALERT — likely evaluating alternatives. Personal outreach within 24h. |

## Win-Back Sequences (after churn)

### Sequence 1: Soft Re-engagement (Day 7-14 inactive)
- Email 1 (Day 7): "We miss you" — show what they're missing (new features, activity from their network)
- Email 2 (Day 10): Value reminder — their data/progress is waiting. "You've built X, don't lose it."
- Email 3 (Day 14): Direct ask — "What went wrong? Reply to this email." (qualitative feedback)

### Sequence 2: Hard Win-Back (Day 30+ inactive)
- Email 1 (Day 30): "Things have changed" — product update summary, new features
- Email 2 (Day 45): Incentive — free month, upgrade offer, exclusive feature access
- Email 3 (Day 60): Final email — "We're keeping your data safe. Come back anytime." Then suppress.

### Rules for Win-Back
- Never send more than 3 win-back emails. After that, suppress and move to cold re-engagement (quarterly).
- Always include unsubscribe. Pestering churned users damages brand.
- Personalize based on their usage history — generic "we miss you" emails convert <1%.
- Track win-back conversion rate by sequence. If <5% for any sequence, rewrite it.

## Retention Metrics Dashboard

| Metric | Formula | Target | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| D1 Retention | Users active Day 1 / Signups | >60% | Weekly |
| D7 Retention | Users active Day 7 / Signups | >30% | Weekly |
| D30 Retention | Users active Day 30 / Signups | >15% | Monthly |
| Churn Rate | Lost users / Start-of-period users | <5% monthly | Monthly |
| NPS | Promoters - Detractors | >40 | Quarterly |
| Resurrection Rate | Reactivated / Total churned | >10% | Monthly |

## Rules
- Measure retention by cohort, not aggregate. Aggregate retention masks trends.
- Never optimize retention without understanding WHY users leave. Survey churned users.
- The best retention feature is a great core product. No amount of emails fixes a bad experience.
- Retention tactics compound — each one adds 2-5%. Stack them.
- Win-back is 5-10x cheaper than new acquisition. Prioritize it.
