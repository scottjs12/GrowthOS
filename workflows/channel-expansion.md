# Channel Expansion Workflow

> Adding a new acquisition channel. High-stakes — wrong channel choice wastes months. This workflow validates before committing.

## Trigger
- Current channels reaching diminishing returns (CPA rising >20% over 4 weeks)
- Strategist identifies new channel opportunity
- ICP research reveals audience concentration on untapped platform
- Competitive intel shows competitor success on a channel you're not using

## Agent Chain

STEP 1: PM — Channel Evaluation Brief
- Input: Channel hypothesis + strategic rationale
- Output: Channel evaluation brief: why this channel, estimated audience size, competitive presence, resource requirements, success criteria
- Decision gate: Strategist approves channel test (budget + timeline commitment)
- Rules: Never test more than 1 new channel per quarter. Focus beats diversification.

STEP 2: Researcher — Channel Validation
- Input: Channel evaluation brief
- Output: Audience validation (is the ICP actually there?), competitive landscape on that channel, content/ad format requirements, community norms and anti-patterns
- Rules: Don't just confirm the hypothesis — actively look for reasons it might fail. Flag disqualifiers.
- Handoff: Validation → PM (go/no-go decision)

STEP 3: PM — Go/No-Go Decision
- Decision gate: Based on Researcher validation
- Go: Issue briefs to Content Writer + Designer + Paid + Ops
- No-go: Document why, archive for future reconsideration
- Rules: "Maybe" is not a decision. Either commit for 90 days or don't start.

STEP 4: Content Writer + Designer — Channel-Specific Assets
- Input: Channel brief with format specs, audience context, ICP angles
- Output: Channel-native content (copy + creative adapted to platform norms)
- Rules: Don't repurpose existing creative 1:1. Adapt to channel norms. What works on Meta won't work on LinkedIn.

STEP 5: Paid — Campaign Setup (if paid channel)
- Input: Assets + audience research + budget allocation
- Output: Campaign structure, targeting, bid strategy, UTMs
- Rules: Start with minimum viable budget. Set 30-day evaluation window. Define kill criteria upfront.
- Reference: `00_shared/briefs/media-buyer-brief-template.md`

STEP 6: Ops — Full Trafficking
- Input: Everything from Steps 4-5
- Output: Full trafficking checklist (new channel = full QA, not abbreviated)
- Decision gate: HARD GATE
- Reference: `09_growth-ops/launch-checklist.md`

STEP 7: Analytics — Tracking Setup
- Input: New channel UTMs, conversion events, attribution requirements
- Output: Channel tracking confirmed, dashboard updated with new channel section
- Rules: Verify attribution model handles the new channel correctly. Set up channel-specific conversion tracking.

STEP 8: Launch + 90-Day Evaluation
- Day 1-7: Learning phase (algorithm optimizing, no decisions)
- Day 7-30: Optimize (adjust targeting, creative, bid strategy)
- Day 30-60: Scale or cut (is CPA trending toward target?)
- Day 60-90: Final verdict (scale, maintain, or kill)

## Exit Criteria (at 90 days)
- [ ] Channel delivers CPA within 20% of target — KEEP and scale
- [ ] Channel CPA >20% above target but improving — extend 30 days
- [ ] Channel CPA >50% above target with no improvement trend — KILL
- [ ] All learnings documented regardless of outcome
- [ ] Pattern library updated if transferable insight found

## Rules
- 90-day commitment minimum — don't kill a channel in week 2
- One new channel at a time — spreading thin guarantees failure
- Channel-native content only — repurposed assets underperform by 40-60%
- Kill criteria defined before launch — never after
