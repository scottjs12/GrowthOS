# Competitive Response Workflow

> Most competitor moves don't require a response. The default is "monitor and continue." This workflow is for when you need to act.

## Trigger
- Major competitor launches new product/feature
- Competitor raises significant funding round
- Competitor slashes pricing or changes model
- Competitor runs aggressive campaign targeting your users
- Market analyst/press coverage shifts narrative

## Severity Assessment (before responding)

| Signal | Severity | Default Action |
|---|---|---|
| Competitor ships feature you already have | LOW | Accelerate your messaging. You already won. |
| Competitor ships feature you don't have | MEDIUM | Monitor adoption. Only respond if users ask about it. |
| Competitor cuts pricing aggressively | MEDIUM | Do NOT match. Compete on value. |
| Competitor directly targets your users (ads, campaigns) | HIGH | Defend: retarget your users, strengthen positioning. |
| Competitor raises large round / gains major press | LOW | Ignore the funding. Monitor what they do with it. |
| Users/prospects mention competitor in churn surveys | HIGH | Respond with differentiation campaign + retention messaging. |

Rule: "Monitor and continue" is the right response 70% of the time. Only activate this workflow for MEDIUM-HIGH severity.

## Agent Chain (when action is needed)

### STEP 1: Researcher — Rapid Intel (0-24h)
- What exactly launched/changed?
- What's the user/market reaction? (social, reviews, forums)
- How does it compare to your offering? (feature parity, pricing, positioning)
- Output: 1-page competitive brief with recommendation: respond / monitor / ignore

### STEP 2: PM — Decision (24-48h)
- Review competitive brief with Strategist
- Decision: Respond (activate steps 3-5) or Monitor (set 30-day check-in)
- If responding, define scope: messaging only? campaign? feature acceleration?

### STEP 3: Content Writer — Response Messaging (48-72h)
- Draft differentiation messaging (never attack — differentiate)
- Update comparison page / landing page if one exists
- Prepare sales enablement talking points
- Rules: Lead with your strengths, not their weaknesses. "We do X better" not "They can't do X."

### STEP 4: Paid + SEO — Distribution (72h-1 week)
- Paid: Adjust targeting if competitor is bidding on your brand terms. Consider competitor keyword campaigns.
- SEO: Publish comparison content if search volume warrants it. Update existing competitive pages.
- CRM: Send retention-focused messaging to at-risk segments (users who match competitor's target profile)

### STEP 5: Analytics — Monitor Impact (1-4 weeks)
- Track: churn rate, signup rate, brand search volume, competitor mention frequency
- Report: Did the competitor move actually impact your metrics?
- If no impact after 4 weeks: archive and return to normal operations
- If impact detected: escalate to Strategist for strategic response

## Exit Criteria
- [ ] Competitive brief filed by Researcher
- [ ] Decision documented (respond/monitor/ignore)
- [ ] If responding: messaging deployed, campaigns adjusted
- [ ] 4-week impact assessment completed
- [ ] Pattern library updated if transferable learning

## Decision Principles
1. **Never respond to funding announcements.** Money is potential, not product.
2. **Never compete on price.** Price wars destroy margins. Compete on value.
3. **Differentiate, don't attack.** "We're better at X" not "They suck at X."
4. **Speed matters for messaging, not panic.** Take 48h to craft a good response rather than 4h to craft a reactive one.
5. **Most competitive moves are noise.** Only signal matters — and signal shows up in YOUR metrics, not their press releases.

## Rules
- The best competitive response is shipping faster. Product velocity beats marketing response.
- Update your comparison/alternative pages quarterly regardless of competitive moves. This is SEO hygiene.
- Track competitor product changes monthly in a simple log. Most moves are incremental — the pattern matters more than individual moves.
- Never let competitor anxiety drive your roadmap. Your customers' needs > your competitor's features.
