# B2B vs B2C Adaptation Guide

> GrowthOS works for both B2B and B2C. The universal skills are the same — what changes is emphasis, channels, and metrics. This guide shows you where the system flexes.

---

## The Core Difference

**B2C:** High volume, low touch, fast decisions. Optimize for speed-to-value and habit formation.
**B2B:** Lower volume, high touch, longer cycles. Optimize for pipeline velocity and deal qualification.

The GrowthOS agents handle both — you just tune the CLIENT CONTEXT differently.

## Agent-by-Agent Adaptation

### Researcher (`01`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| ICP definition | Demographics, psychographics, behaviors | Firmographics, job title, buying committee |
| Research sources | Reddit, TikTok comments, app reviews, surveys | LinkedIn, G2/Capterra reviews, sales call transcripts, industry reports |
| Competitive intel | Feature comparison, pricing, app store positioning | Positioning, sales objections, win/loss analysis |
| Psychology toolkit | Impulse triggers, social proof, FOMO | Authority, risk reduction, consensus building |

### Content Writer (`02`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Primary formats | Social posts, short-form video scripts, ad copy, push notifications | Blog posts, case studies, white papers, email nurture, LinkedIn posts |
| Email style | Short, punchy, one CTA, mobile-first | Longer-form, educational, multi-stakeholder awareness |
| Cold email | Rarely used (product-led) | Critical channel — OPPA framework, multi-touch sequences |
| Social content | TikTok/Instagram-first, UGC, entertainment | LinkedIn-first, thought leadership, industry insights |
| Voice | Casual, peer-to-peer, emoji-friendly | Professional, insight-driven, credibility-focused |

### SEO (`03`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Keyword intent | Transactional + informational (how-to, reviews, comparisons) | Commercial + informational (guides, frameworks, benchmarks) |
| Content types | Listicles, product comparisons, how-to videos | In-depth guides, original research, industry reports |
| pSEO opportunities | "[Product] vs [Alternative]", location pages, directory listings | Integration pages, use case pages, industry verticals |
| ASO | Critical (app store optimization) | Rarely applicable |
| AI-SEO (AEO) | Consumer queries, product recommendations | Business queries, vendor comparisons, "best tool for X" |

### Designer (`04`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Ad creative | Scroll-stopping visuals, UGC, lifestyle imagery, bold colors | Clean, professional, data-driven visuals, subtle brand |
| Angles | Pain, identity, social proof, UGC, before/after | Demo, social proof (logos), comparison, case study |
| Landing pages | Short, punchy, above-the-fold CTA, mobile-first | Longer, educational, form-based, trust signals |
| Video | Short-form (15-30s), entertainment-adjacent | Demo videos (60-90s), webinar clips, customer testimonials |

### CRO (`05`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Primary conversion | Signup / purchase / app install | Demo request / free trial / contact sales |
| Test velocity | High (large sample sizes, short cycles) | Low (small samples, need longer test windows) |
| Key flows to optimize | Onboarding, checkout, paywall | Demo booking, trial activation, pricing page |
| Guardrails | Engagement, session duration, feature adoption | Lead quality score, SQL rate, pipeline value |

### Paid (`06`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Primary platforms | Meta, TikTok, Google (Search + PMax), YouTube | Google Search, LinkedIn, Meta (retargeting only), YouTube |
| Targeting | Interest + behavioral, broad, LAL | Job title, company size, industry, ABM lists |
| Budget split | 70% prospecting / 30% retargeting | 50% prospecting / 50% retargeting + nurture |
| Bidding | Lowest cost, optimize for conversions | Cost cap, optimize for lead quality (not volume) |
| Retargeting | Hot/warm/cold windows, frequency-based | Account-based, multi-touch, sales-assisted |
| Video | Short-form, hook-first, entertainment | Demo-style, problem-solution, longer form |

### Analytics (`07`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| North Star | DAU, WAU, or activated users | Teams/accounts with active usage, or pipeline value |
| Funnel | Visit → Signup → Activate → Retain → Pay → Refer | Visit → Lead → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Close |
| Key metrics | Activation rate, D7/D30 retention, viral coefficient, ARPU | MQL→SQL rate, pipeline velocity, deal size, CAC payback |
| Attribution | Last-touch (fast cycles, high volume) | Multi-touch (long cycles, many touchpoints) |
| Tools | Mixpanel/Amplitude (product analytics) + GA4 | HubSpot/Salesforce (CRM) + GA4 + Segment |

### CRM (`08`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Lifecycle model | Signup → Activate → Retain → Refer | Lead → MQL → SQL → Customer → Expand |
| Email sequences | Onboarding, re-engagement, milestone, referral | Nurture, demo follow-up, onboarding, expansion |
| Segmentation | Behavioral (usage, engagement, lifecycle stage) | Firmographic (company size, industry) + behavioral |
| Referral program | Viral loops, two-sided incentives, social sharing | Partner programs, co-marketing, customer advocacy |
| Community | Discord, Reddit, social engagement | Slack communities, user groups, customer advisory boards |

### Ops (`09`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| RevOps | Light — product-led, self-serve | Critical — lead scoring, MQL/SQL handoff, speed-to-lead |
| Tool stack | ESP + product analytics + ad platforms | CRM + marketing automation + ad platforms + sales tools |
| Launch cadence | Fast — weekly iterations | Slower — monthly campaigns, quarterly programs |
| Tracking complexity | Event-based, product analytics | Multi-system (CRM + analytics + ad platforms + sales) |

### Engineer (`10`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Landing pages | Fast, mobile-first, app store CTAs | Form-heavy, multi-page, integration with CRM |
| Tracking | Product events, app analytics, pixel setup | CRM integration, server-side tracking, lead capture |
| A/B testing | Client-side, high traffic, fast results | Server-side, lower traffic, need patience |

### Influencer/Partnerships (`11`)

| Dimension | B2C Emphasis | B2B Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| Creator type | Lifestyle influencers, micro-creators, UGC | Industry thought leaders, analysts, podcast hosts |
| Platforms | TikTok, Instagram, YouTube | LinkedIn, podcasts, webinars, conferences |
| Partnership model | Affiliate, gifting, sponsored content | Co-marketing, integration partnerships, channel partnerships |
| Metrics | Reach, engagement, CPA via affiliate links | Pipeline influenced, leads generated, brand authority |

## Quick Decision: Which Model Am I?

**You're B2C if:**
- Users sign up and get value without talking to anyone
- Your price point is under $50/mo per user
- Decisions are made by individuals, not committees
- Your growth loop is product → content → paid → viral

**You're B2B if:**
- A salesperson or demo is involved in most deals
- Your price point is $50+/mo per seat or $500+/mo per account
- Buying involves multiple stakeholders
- Your growth loop is content → lead gen → nurture → sales → expand

**You're a hybrid if:**
- Product-led growth with a sales-assisted motion (PLG + Sales)
- Free tier for individuals, paid tier for teams
- Self-serve below $X/mo, sales-assisted above

For hybrids: fill out CLIENT CONTEXT twice — once for the self-serve motion (B2C playbook) and once for the sales-assisted motion (B2B playbook). The PM agent coordinates between them.

## What Stays the Same

Regardless of B2B or B2C, these universal skills don't change:
- How to write a hypothesis
- How to design a statistically valid test
- How to structure a brief
- How to name events
- How to run a post-mortem
- How to do keyword research
- How to build a modular template system
- How to manage a sprint

The craft is the craft. The context adapts.
