# Enterprise & Sales-Assisted Growth Playbook

> Not every customer self-serves. Enterprise deals require sales involvement, but the best enterprise motion starts with product-led adoption. This playbook covers the hybrid: PLG for land, sales for expand.

## The Land-and-Expand Model

```
LAND (PLG)                           EXPAND (Sales-Assisted)
──────────                           ──────────────────────
Individual discovers product         Usage grows → triggers PQL
  ↓                                     ↓
Self-serve signup (free/trial)       Sales outreach (within 24h)
  ↓                                     ↓
Activates → builds habit             Sales qualifies (team size, budget, use case)
  ↓                                     ↓
Invites 2-3 teammates                 Sales proposes team/enterprise plan
  ↓                                     ↓
Small team adopts                    Procurement/legal/security review
  ↓                                     ↓
Organic word-of-mouth in org         Enterprise contract closed
  ↓                                     ↓
Champions emerge                     Implementation + dedicated onboarding
```

## Product-Qualified Leads (PQLs)

### Defining PQL Signals
PQLs are based on product behavior, not form fills. Define scoring based on:

| Signal | Points | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Invited 3+ team members | 20 | Team adoption = expansion opportunity |
| Used product 5+ days in last 14 | 15 | Regular usage = real value |
| Explored enterprise features (SSO, admin, API) | 15 | Evaluating upgrade path |
| Exported/shared output externally | 10 | Creating workflow dependency |
| Visited pricing page 2+ times | 10 | Purchase intent |
| Connected integration (Slack, API, etc.) | 10 | Embedding in workflow |
| Hit usage limit (storage, exports, seats) | 20 | Natural upgrade moment |

### PQL Threshold
- Score ≥ 40: Route to sales immediately (within 24h)
- Score 25-39: Add to nurture track. Monitor for 2 weeks.
- Score < 25: Stay in PLG funnel. No sales touch.

### Rules
- PQLs convert 5-8x better than MQLs. If you have a PLG product, PQLs should be your primary sales pipeline.
- Speed-to-lead matters: Contact PQLs within 24h. Conversion rate drops 50% after 48h.
- Sales should reference the user's actual product usage in outreach. "I noticed your team has been using [feature]..." not "Would you like a demo?"

## Enterprise Sales Cycle

### Stakeholders
| Role | Cares About | Your Approach |
|---|---|---|
| End user (champion) | Product quality, ease of use | Already sold — they're using it. Enable them to sell internally. |
| Team lead | Productivity, cost savings, team adoption | ROI calculator, usage reports showing team value |
| IT/Security | SSO, SOC 2, data residency, permissions | Security whitepaper, compliance docs, admin controls demo |
| Procurement | Contract terms, pricing, vendor risk | Standard enterprise agreement, flexible billing |
| Finance/CFO | Total cost, ROI, budget allocation | Business case template, TCO comparison |

### Champion Enablement
Your internal champion is your best salesperson. Give them:
- [ ] One-page internal pitch deck (problem → solution → results → ask)
- [ ] ROI calculator (input team size and usage → output time/money saved)
- [ ] Usage dashboard (shows team adoption metrics they can share with leadership)
- [ ] Case studies from similar companies/industries
- [ ] Security/compliance FAQ for IT review
- Rule: Make it easy for champions to sell internally. If they have to build their own business case, you've already lost half of them.

### Enterprise Pricing Framework
| Model | When to Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Per-seat | Team adoption is the growth vector | Slack, Figma, Notion |
| Usage-based | Value scales with consumption | AWS, Twilio, OpenAI |
| Tiered (features) | Clear feature differentiation by segment | Zoom Free/Pro/Business/Enterprise |
| Hybrid (seat + usage) | Both team size and consumption matter | Snowflake, Datadog |

- Rule: Pricing should align with the value metric — the unit of value the customer receives. If value = outputs created, price per output. If value = team productivity, price per seat.
- Rule: Enterprise pricing should be 3-5x the self-serve price for equivalent usage. The premium covers: SLA, support, security, customization.
- Rule: Never discount more than 20% off list price. Discounts above 20% signal that your pricing is wrong, not that the deal is special.

## Sales-Assisted Metrics

| Metric | Target | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| PQL → SQL conversion | >30% | Weekly |
| SQL → Closed Won | >20% | Monthly |
| Average deal size | Increasing QoQ | Quarterly |
| Sales cycle length | <45 days for mid-market, <90 days enterprise | Monthly |
| Net revenue retention | >120% | Monthly |
| Expansion rate (existing accounts) | >30% annually | Quarterly |

## Handoff: Growth → Sales

### What Growth Owns
- Awareness → Signup → Activation → PQL identification
- Champion enablement materials
- Product usage data for sales context
- Case studies and social proof

### What Sales Owns
- PQL outreach and qualification
- Demo and enterprise evaluation
- Procurement/legal/security process
- Contract negotiation and close
- Dedicated onboarding for enterprise accounts

### The Handoff Moment
- Growth identifies PQL based on scoring
- Growth passes to Sales with: user profile, product usage summary, team size, features used, engagement score
- Sales contacts within 24h referencing actual usage
- Rule: The handoff should feel seamless to the user. They should not feel "passed off" — they should feel "graduated to VIP treatment."

## Rules
- PLG and sales are not competing motions — they're sequential. PLG lands, sales expands.
- Never gate basic product functionality to force enterprise sales conversations. Users will leave.
- Enterprise sales should follow product adoption, not precede it. Don't cold-call companies that aren't using the product.
- Track self-serve revenue and sales-assisted revenue separately. Mixing them hides the true cost of sales.
- The best enterprise deals close themselves. Your job is to remove friction from the internal buying process, not to "sell."
