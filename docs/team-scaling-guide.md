# Team Scaling Guide

> The right hire at the wrong time is the wrong hire. This guide covers when to add each role, how team structure evolves, and what breaks at each stage.

## Stage 1: Solo Operator (1 person, $0-50K/yr spend)

### Who you are
- Founder, solo growth lead, or first marketing hire
- You are the PM, Researcher, Content Writer, and Paid agent simultaneously

### Which GrowthOS agents to use
- **Always active:** PM (you), Researcher, Content Writer
- **Activate when needed:** Paid, Analytics, Ops
- **Skip for now:** Designer (use Canva/templates), CRO (not enough traffic to test), Engineer (use no-code), SEO (takes 6+ months), Influencer, CRM

### What breaks at this stage
- Context switching kills depth. You'll write ad copy after checking analytics after adjusting bids.
- Mitigation: Use the weekly runbook. Batch by day (Monday = review, Tuesday = create, Wednesday = ship).

### When to hire your first person
- Signal: You're spending >$20K/mo on ads and can't optimize because you're creating content
- Signal: You have >1,000 users and no lifecycle emails
- Signal: You're working >30 hrs/week on growth and still falling behind

## Stage 2: Small Team (2-4 people, $50-250K/yr spend)

### Recommended hires (in order)
1. **Content + Design hybrid** — Someone who can write AND design. This is your highest-leverage hire because they unblock the creative production bottleneck.
2. **Paid/Performance marketer** — When ad spend exceeds $10K/mo, a dedicated person pays for themselves in optimization gains.
3. **Analytics/Data** — When you have >5K users and need cohort analysis, attribution, and experiment rigor.

### GrowthOS agents active
- All of Stage 1 plus: Designer, Paid, Analytics, Ops
- Starting to activate: CRO (you now have enough traffic), CRM

### Team structure

```
Growth Lead (you)
  ├── Content + Design
  ├── Paid/Performance
  └── Data/Analytics (part-time or contractor OK)
```

### What breaks at this stage
- Handoffs between people are informal. Work gets lost in Slack.
- Mitigation: Start using the handoff confirmation protocol. Brief → receipt → start → complete.
- The growth lead becomes a bottleneck. Everything routes through you.
- Mitigation: Start delegating decision rights. Paid can adjust budgets <20%. Content can publish without approval.

### When to hire more
- Signal: Experiment velocity is <2 tests/month (you need a CRO or dedicated engineer)
- Signal: Organic is untapped and paid CAC is rising (hire SEO)
- Signal: Churn is >5% monthly and you have no lifecycle automation (hire CRM)

---

## Stage 3: Growth Team (5-10 people, $250K-1M/yr spend)

### Recommended hires (in order)
4. **Growth Engineer** — Build landing pages, instrument A/B tests, implement tracking. Unblocks CRO velocity.
5. **CRM/Lifecycle** — Onboarding sequences, retention flows, segmentation. The retention investment starts paying back.
6. **SEO** — If organic is a viable channel. SEO compounds but takes 6-12 months to show ROI.
7. **CRO** — Dedicated hypothesis generation, test design, learning synthesis. Only if you have >50K monthly visitors.

### GrowthOS agents active
- All 13 agents are active
- PM is now a dedicated person, not the growth lead wearing a PM hat

### Team structure

```
VP/Head of Growth
  ├── Growth PM
  ├── Content Writer
  ├── Designer
  ├── Paid/Performance
  ├── Growth Engineer
  ├── CRM/Lifecycle
  ├── Data/Analytics
  └── SEO (or contractor)
```

### What breaks at this stage
- The PM becomes overwhelmed coordinating 7+ people
- Mitigation: Split into pods. Acquisition pod (Paid + Content + Designer) and Retention pod (CRM + CRO + Engineer). PM coordinates across pods.
- Experiments compete for traffic and resources
- Mitigation: PM manages experiment backlog with ICE scoring. Max 3 concurrent tests.
- Strategic vs. tactical tension — VP wants to think big, team needs day-to-day direction
- Mitigation: VP becomes the Strategist (weekly sync, not daily). PM runs day-to-day.

### When to scale beyond 10
- Signal: You're in 3+ channels and can't give each proper attention
- Signal: International expansion requires localized content/creative
- Signal: Enterprise sales motion requires dedicated sales enablement

## Stage 4: Scaled Team (10-20 people, $1-5M/yr spend)

### Additional hires
8. **Team leads** — Acquisition lead, Retention lead, Content lead
9. **Influencer/Partnerships** — When creator economy is a viable channel
10. **Second designer or video specialist** — Creative volume demands it
11. **Marketing ops / RevOps** — Dedicated tool stack management, integrations, data pipelines

### Team structure

```
VP of Growth
  ├── Head of Acquisition
  │     ├── Paid (1-2 people)
  │     ├── SEO
  │     ├── Content (1-2 people)
  │     └── Influencer
  ├── Head of Retention
  │     ├── CRM/Lifecycle
  │     ├── CRO
  │     └── Community
  ├── Growth Engineering (2-3 people)
  ├── Growth Analytics (1-2 people)
  └── Growth Ops / RevOps
```

### What breaks at this stage
- Communication overhead grows quadratically. 15 people = 105 communication channels.
- Mitigation: Pod structure with leads. Cross-pod sync weekly (30 min), not daily.
- GrowthOS weekly rhythm needs to adapt — PM can't sync with 15 individuals
- Mitigation: PM syncs with pod leads. Pod leads sync with their teams.
- Attribution becomes political. "Who gets credit for this conversion?"
- Mitigation: Use the Analytics attribution framework. Agree on model before campaigns launch.

---

## Stage 5: Growth Organization (20+ people, $5M+/yr spend)

### What changes
- Growth is now a department, not a team
- You need: directors, managers, specialists, analysts
- GrowthOS becomes the operating framework, not the org chart
- Each pod runs its own weekly rhythm; VP reviews monthly

### GrowthOS adaptation
- STRATEGIST.md → VP of Growth
- PM AGENT.md → Multiple PMs (one per pod or major initiative)
- AGENT.md files → Competency frameworks for hiring and performance reviews
- Workflows → Team SOPs (adapted per pod)
- Client context → Business unit or product line context

### What breaks at this stage
- The system was designed for flat teams. You need hierarchy.
- Mitigation: Add a "leads" layer. AGENT.md defines the craft; the lead manages the people.
- Experimentation velocity paradoxically slows (more process, more stakeholders)
- Mitigation: Empower pod leads to approve and run experiments within their domain. Only cross-pod experiments need VP approval.

## Hiring Principles

- Hire for the bottleneck, not the vision. If content is the bottleneck, hire content — not an influencer manager for a program you'll launch in Q3.
- Generalists first, specialists later. Your first 4 hires should each cover 2-3 agent roles.
- Hire people who can use AI tools. GrowthOS is designed for AI-augmented work. A content writer who uses AI effectively produces 3x the output.
- Never hire for a channel you haven't validated. Run the channel for 90 days with existing team first. Only hire when it works and needs dedicated attention.
- Contractors before FTEs for specialist roles (SEO, video, influencer) until the channel is proven.

## Rules

- Don't hire ahead of the curve. Each hire should solve a problem you have today, not one you might have in 6 months.
- Every new hire should be able to point to which GrowthOS agent(s) they own. If a hire doesn't map to an agent, question whether the role is needed.
- Re-read the relevant AGENT.md before writing any job description. The "You own" and "Universal Skills" sections are your JD outline.
- When in doubt, hire a strong generalist over a narrow specialist. Generalists adapt; specialists get stranded when priorities shift.
