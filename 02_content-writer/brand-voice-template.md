## WHY THIS MATTERS

Without a completed voice guide, every piece of copy requires subjective back-and-forth that wastes cycles. "I don't love the tone" is not actionable feedback — but "this violates our jargon policy" is. A filled-out voice doc turns taste into rules, and rules into speed.

## 2. TONE SPECTRUM

Tone is not the same as voice. Voice is who we are. Tone is how we adjust based on context. The voice stays constant — the tone shifts.

**Rules:**
- Never let tone shift so far that the brand becomes unrecognizable across channels
- The shift is in intensity and formality, not in personality
- When in doubt, default to the voice adjectives above

| Channel | Tone Shift | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Paid ads (cold)** | [e.g., "Punchiest. Lead with tension. No softening."] | [e.g., "Your spreadsheet isn't a CRM. It's a graveyard."] |
| **Paid ads (retargeting)** | [e.g., "Warmer. They know us. Speak to their hesitation."] | [e.g., "Still thinking about it? Here's what changed for [Customer]."] |
| **Landing pages** | [e.g., "Confident and proof-heavy. No fluff. Every sentence earns its place."] | [e.g., "Cut onboarding time by 60%. Here's how."] |
| **Email — nurture** | [e.g., "Conversational. One idea per email. Feels like a note, not a newsletter."] | [e.g., "Quick question — what's your biggest bottleneck right now?"] |
| **Email — transactional** | [e.g., "Clear, minimal, zero personality required."] | [e.g., "Your invoice is attached. Questions? Reply here."] |
| **Organic social** | [e.g., "Most casual. Opinionated. Shareable. POV-driven."] | [e.g., "Hot take: your brand guidelines doc is 40 pages nobody reads."] |
| **Support / help docs** | [e.g., "Patient and precise. Never condescending. Assume competence."] | [e.g., "To fix this, go to Settings > Integrations > Reconnect."] |

- **Complexity rule:** [e.g., "One idea per sentence. If a sentence has a semicolon, split it. If it has three commas, rewrite it."]

## 6. REVIEW CHECKLIST

Run every piece of copy through this checklist before it ships. If any box is unchecked, rewrite.

### Voice alignment
- [ ] Does this sound like our brand personality adjectives? Read it aloud — would you recognize the brand without seeing the logo?
- [ ] Does it pass the "We sound like X, not Y" test for all three contrast pairs?
- [ ] Could a competitor have written this? If yes, it's not distinctive enough. Rewrite.

### Tone calibration
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the channel and audience state (cold/warm/hot)?
- [ ] If this is an ad, is it punchy enough? If this is support copy, is it patient enough?

### Language rules
- [ ] Zero banned words or phrases?
- [ ] Jargon matches the audience — technical terms for practitioners, plain language for buyers?
- [ ] Sentence length within guidelines? No run-on sentences?
- [ ] Formatting preferences followed (contractions, Oxford comma, capitalization, numbers)?

### Clarity and specificity
- [ ] Every sentence earns its place? Delete any line and re-read — if nothing is lost, cut it.
- [ ] Specific over vague? Numbers over adjectives? Proof over claims?
- [ ] One idea per sentence? One job per asset?

### Audience fit
- [ ] Written for the specific ICP segment, not a generic "everyone" audience?
- [ ] Proof points match what this audience values?
- [ ] Vocabulary matches their fluency level — not too simple, not too jargon-heavy?
