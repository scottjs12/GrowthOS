# Social Content Batch Generator

> Produces a full week of social posts across platforms. Uses Content Writer social methodology + content pillar strategy.

## What You Get
- 5-7 days of social content across chosen platforms
- Posts organized by content pillar
- Platform-specific formatting (character counts, hashtags, hooks)
- Mix of formats: text, carousel ideas, video concepts, stories

## What You Need First
- [ ] ICP profile (who follows you?)
- [ ] Brand voice guidelines (tone, personality)
- [ ] Content pillars (3-5 themes your content rotates through)
- [ ] Platforms: LinkedIn / Twitter/X / Instagram / TikTok
- [ ] Any upcoming launches, events, or timely hooks
- [ ] Recent high-performing posts (optional — helps identify what works)

## Prompt Chain

### Step 1: Load Context
```
Load the Content Writer agent: @02_content-writer/AGENT.md
Also reference: @02_content-writer/social-content-playbook.md

ICP:
[Paste ICP profile]

Brand voice: [Professional / Casual / Playful / Technical / Authoritative — with specific notes]

Content pillars:
1. [Pillar — e.g., Product tips and tutorials]
2. [Pillar — e.g., Industry insights and trends]
3. [Pillar — e.g., Customer stories and social proof]
4. [Pillar — e.g., Behind-the-scenes and culture]
5. [Pillar — e.g., Hot takes and thought leadership]

Platforms: [LinkedIn / Twitter / Instagram / TikTok]
Week of: [Date]
Timely hooks: [Any launches, events, trending topics to leverage]
```

### Step 2: Plan the Week
```
Create a content calendar for this week. Map each day to:
- Content pillar
- Platform(s)
- Format (text post / carousel / short video / story / thread)
- Goal (engagement / reach / traffic / conversion)

Rules:
- Never post the same pillar two days in a row
- Mix formats — no more than 2 text-only posts per week
- At least 1 post should drive traffic (link to content, product, or landing page)
- At least 1 post should be pure engagement (question, poll, hot take — no links)
- Weekend posts optional — only if your ICP is active on weekends

Present as a table:
Day | Pillar | Platform | Format | Goal
```

### Step 3: Write the Posts
```
Now write each post for the week.

Per platform formatting:

LinkedIn:
- Hook line (first line visible before "see more" — make it count)
- Body (short paragraphs, use line breaks aggressively)
- CTA or engagement prompt at the end
- 1,300 chars ideal, 3,000 max

Twitter/X:
- Single tweet: 280 chars max. Front-load the hook.
- Thread: 3-7 tweets. First tweet is the hook, last tweet is the CTA.

Instagram:
- Caption: Hook in first line, body, hashtags (15-20 relevant)
- Carousel: Slide-by-slide text content (6-10 slides, first slide is the hook)

TikTok:
- Hook text (on-screen, 3 seconds)
- Script outline (15-30 seconds)
- Caption with hashtags

For every post:
- The hook must work in isolation (would someone stop scrolling?)
- Every sentence earns its place
- CTA is specific when included
```

### Step 4: Review
```
Review the full week:

1. Is there a mix of pillars? (No pillar repeated consecutively)
2. Is there format variety? (Not all text posts)
3. Does each hook stop the scroll?
4. Are engagement posts truly engaging? (Would YOU comment?)
5. Are link posts providing genuine value before asking for the click?
6. Does the overall week feel like a person, not a brand?

Rewrite any post that feels generic or could have come from any brand.
```

## Tips
- Batch-produce weekly (every Monday or Friday). Reactive posts are the only exception.
- Track performance in Analytics — share data monthly with the team
- Save winning posts in a swipe file for future reference
- Repurpose: A LinkedIn post can become a Twitter thread, an Instagram carousel, and a TikTok script
